# RENTFLOW V1 - FOUNDATIONAL PROPTECH PLATFORM FOR AFRICA

You are a Principal Software Architect, Product Manager, Staff Backend Engineer, Staff Frontend Engineer, SaaS Architect, Security Engineer, DevOps Engineer, and UX Designer.

Your task is to design and build a production-grade multi-tenant SaaS platform called RentFlow.

Do not build a simple property management system.

Build a scalable rental operations platform focused on rent payment reconciliation, occupancy tracking, lease management, and future financial automation.

The platform must be capable of scaling from:

* 10 units
* 100 units
* 1,000 units
* 50,000+ units

without requiring a database redesign.

---

# PRODUCT VISION

RentFlow helps landlords, property managers, and real estate agencies track rent payments and occupancy without relying on screenshots, spreadsheets, or manual reconciliation.

The MVP does NOT process money.

The MVP does NOT hold funds.

The MVP tracks payments, verifies payments, manages tenants, manages leases, and creates a foundation for future payment automation.

The primary customer is:

Property Managers

Secondary customers:

Landlords

The entire architecture should optimize for property management companies managing hundreds or thousands of units.

# SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

Use a multi-tenant SaaS architecture.

Core Structure:

Organization
├── Users
├── Properties
├── Blocks
├── Units
├── Leases
├── Tenants
├── Payments
├── Notifications
└── Reports

Every business record belongs to an organization.

No organization should ever access another organization's data.

All queries must be tenant-scoped.

---

# USER ROLES

1. Super Admin

Platform owner.

Can access platform-wide analytics.

Cannot modify organization data.

---

2. Organization Owner

Property company owner.

Full access within organization.

---

3. Property Manager

Manages properties, leases, tenants, and payments.

---

4. Accountant

Can verify payments.

Can view financial reports.

Cannot manage users.

---

5. Caretaker

Can view occupancy.

Can update unit status.

Cannot view sensitive financial data.

---

6. Tenant

Accesses tenant portal.

Can submit payments.

Can view rent status.

3. Create leases
4. Invite tenants
5. Receive payment submissions
6. Verify payments
7. Track rent collection status
